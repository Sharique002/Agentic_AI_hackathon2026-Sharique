BIGGEST CHALLENGE FACED
========================

The hardest problem we solved wasn't technical — it was designing a decision engine 
that is simultaneously autonomous AND safe.

In most AI systems, you pick one: either the system makes decisions autonomously 
(fast, but risky), or it defers everything to humans (safe, but defeats the purpose). 
CARE needed to do both — resolve clear-cut cases instantly while knowing exactly when 
to stop and escalate.

The specific challenge was building a priority-ordered policy chain that handles 
conflicting signals correctly. For example:

- A VIP customer with a pre-approved exception wants to return a product *after* the 
  return window has expired AND the device has been registered online (making it 
  non-returnable). Which policy wins?

- A standard-tier customer claims to be a "premium member" and demands an "instant 
  refund" — a policy that doesn't exist. The system needs to recognize this isn't 
  just a misunderstanding; it's a social engineering attempt.

- A defective product is reported outside the return window, but the warranty is 
  still active. The system can't just refund (wrong policy) or reject (warranty 
  covers it). It needs to escalate to the warranty team — a third path.

Getting the priority ordering right required mapping every edge case in the knowledge 
base to a deterministic decision tree, then testing all 20 tickets to ensure no 
policy leaked into another's domain. The fraud detector alone went through multiple 
iterations — initial versions either over-flagged legitimate complaints or missed 
subtle tier-mismatch attacks.

The breakthrough was treating the decision engine as a layered filter rather than a 
classifier. Each policy check either returns a definitive decision (and exits the 
chain) or passes through to the next layer. This gives us O(1) decision paths with 
clear precedence, and it's trivially auditable because you can trace exactly which 
layer caught each ticket.

The result: 20/20 ticket coverage, zero false positives on fraud, and every decision 
fully explainable — without a single LLM call.
